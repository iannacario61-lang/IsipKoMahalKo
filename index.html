Mental Health Awareness

Understanding Your Isip

Mental health affects how we think, feel, and act — and it shapes our daily lives as Filipinos navigating work, family, and community.

😔
Depression
Characterized by persistent sadness, loss of interest, and low energy. In Filipino culture, often dismissed as "tamad lang" — but it is a real, treatable condition.
😰
Anxiety
Excessive worry, restlessness, and fear that disrupts daily life. Common among Filipino students and OFWs navigating pressure and separation.
🔥
Burnout
"Pagod na pagod na ako" — burnout is not just tiredness. It erodes motivation, identity, and one's sense of purpose over time.
🌀
Trauma & PTSD
The Philippines is frequently affected by typhoons, disasters, and violence. Trauma can resurface years later through flashbacks and emotional numbness.
💔
Suicidal Ideation
Thoughts of self-harm or suicide require immediate support. Stigma prevents many Filipinos from speaking up. "Kamusta ka talaga?" can be life-saving.
🌱
Pathways to Healing
Recovery is possible through therapy, medication, peer support, and community. The Philippines' Mental Health Act (RA 11036) mandates accessible mental health services.
🇵🇭 Did you know? The Philippines passed the Mental Health Act (RA 11036) in 2018, mandating mental health services in all government hospitals and schools. Yet awareness and access remain major barriers — especially in provinces. This research aims to bridge that gap.

Why Your Voice Matters

This study explores the mental health landscape of Filipinos — understanding what we struggle with, how we cope, and what barriers prevent us from seeking help.

The findings will be used to develop recommendations for better mental health support systems in the Philippines, tailored to our unique cultural context.
